Who We Are
Things at Osprey move full circle, starting with the people, resulting in the product and then back to the people for the lifetime of the product. Many of us live in southwest Colorado near Osprey’s headquarters in Cortez. Nestled at the corner of the rugged San Juan Mountains and on the edge of vast sandstone canyon country, this landscape provides us with constant inspiration and a superb testing ground for our packs. Also a small group of us work in our design studio in Mill Valley, CA. Team Vietnam You’ll find the rest of us, often Osprey founder and head designer Mike Pfotenhauer, in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam. Here, surrounded by heat, endless bustle and vibrant energy, we design and build our packs to the exacting standards expected of Osprey. Living in HCM City provides many benefits, including the ability to ensure fair labor standards and face-to-face relationships with the factories that build our packs, cultural enlightenment, and the design inspiration of a cosmopolitan city. Our mission is to create innovative high performance gear that reflects our love of adventure and our devotion to the outdoors. We succeed when we meet the demanding expectation of our most discerning customers and they are proud to use our packs.

New line of osprey packs are expensive, especially this one. So I had high expectations for this bag.

Little side pockets leave alot to be desired. They are not easy to zip/unzip when the pack is on and they are too small to really hold a waterbottle. I was hoping to use these pockets for a water bottle or other items I frequently access like my cell phone. I'm not sure what the hell osprey is doing with these side pockets. This was a complaint on past commuter packs, which is why I bought this one, hoping for an improvement. Additionally, the side straps used to pull the load tight to your body restrict access to the side pockets which makes them even less functional.

The back design does allow for some breeze through, which cuts down drastically on sweating on hot days. It doesn't work as well as I had hoped, but it's the best I've used. Pockets are super tall and not very deep, which is just how the pack is designed in order for the load to stay close to your back. That's good, but I'm not really used to digging around in a tall pocket to find stuff way at the bottom. Storage size is ample, I can fit my books, laptop, plus a change of clothes/shoes and more in this bag. Laptop section also has a small zipper pocket for cords and such which I like.

The inside of the pack and where the fancy mesh thing is that goes on your back is bright neon green. I didn't really know this when I ordered it. I am in professional settings and liked the neutral black color of the bag so for me this is a downside.

Lid lock thingy works pretty good, the bungee is tight and kinda hard to use right now but will probably loosen up.

4.0 out of 5 starsInitial review
By Double M on February 23, 2015
Size: Medium/LargeColor: Black
Just road tested it today lightly packed to work (Apple Mac air 11in.) and $40 dollar worth of small groceries packed.

I'll leave a Follow up review after 10-20 more rides. But for initial Road test today. Here it goes.

1. I'm 5'10 1/2. Fits snug and proper on hips. So my back didn't feel weighed down when carting groceries I.e a half gallon of Milk and eggs to boot. None of them cracked. Huge Plus.

2. Bag is easy to adjust as you ride. Straps are easy to find and fine tune the fit depending on your load. I was able to do this in steady traffic without error. Specifically near my neck to distribute the weight evenly.

3. The back ventilation is far beyond the best I've ever experienced. I'm an avid cyclists. I've used chrome, Timbuktu, etc. messengers and backpacks. None quite felt as comfortable loaded as the Osprey.

4. The compartments and zippers are outstanding. I finally have a bag that is organized. I know where my keys and lock are. Makes for easier deployment off the bike. Things are ready to go.

Now the few cons I have is I wish the 34L had another color inside besides the green. I like the black as most of my bags are, but I understand how the brighter green allows me to see the inside contents better.

Another con is the bag can get top heavy fast and not stand up on ground. Not a deal breaker but something to get used to from my huge roll tops.

Lastly the light Clip tab could be better. Too play to secure light even when off back.

That's all for now. Great bag. Anything less than a 4 star and I'd question if the superb quality is considered. Going to ride with it a few more rides and see how i feel after initial review.

It's an efficient pack. Pricey but a head nod in the right direction.

Size: Medium/LargeColor: Black Verified Purchase
It's a great commuter backpack (DON'T confuse this for a hiking back pack!) that has served me well. I can easily put a laptop in the back compartment, iPad, and fit a change of clothes with shoes which is a tight fight. This is one of the few commuter backpacks where it works well as a standard back pack that can efficiently be used for bike commuting and also looks like a standard back pack to be taken to work without the standard awkward looking commuter bag for biking. It doesn't have the 100% air tight, rubber interior design for weather but it does have a cover that fits in a bottom compartment and is more than enough. I've had some pieces that have come undone and lost such as the bottom belt and a few zipper handles - it may be an isolated incident and it is backed by a 100% guarantee so I have yet to contact their customer service. Over all, I definitely give this back pack high marks.
